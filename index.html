About

The goal of this assignment was to understand, empathize with, and tell the story of users of a particular interface.
To achieve this, I observed and interviewed users of the Coca Cola drink dispenser placed in the Ratty.
Based on the findings, I generated two different personas to best represent the users, and a storyboard to tell one of the personas' story.
Scroll down to read about more details and my findings of the assignment!

Web Page

Bagel Gourmet Café: Menu Page

Why?

I chose this website because the design’s barely responsive for smaller screen sizes, and defies much of a user’s conceptual model when navigating the website.

Key Issues

To first find out how users of the drink dispenser interact with the interface, I observed and interviewed four different users at the Ratty.


Responsiveness

  • When the screen shrinks, the page simply gets cut off horizontally, so that you have to scroll to the end. On a phone, the page display simply shrinks, so the lettering is too small to read.

Conceptual Model

  • Some style choices make certain texts seem like links while they aren’t, and the navigation buttons change location based on the page a user is on. (My conceptual model expects it to stay in one location)
  • Clicking on the logo does not take the user back to home page, which is now a part of most conceptual models of modern websites.

Visual Design

  • Bottom buttons blend in with the background, and so does the link randomly placed at the top left corner.
  • Layout of prices are offset, and so are very hard to read.
  • Hard to tell which options belong to what headers, since the only distinctions are underlined vs. non-underlined (some times italics?)
  • All contents are shifted to the left, with very tight columns and small text. The margins are missing in some text displays, and the color scheme offers little contrast.

Accessibility

  • There is no alt text for the images, so screen readers cannot read them.
  • Only uses h4 and h5 elements, and no h1 is found.
  • Language is never identified
  • Poor color contrast.
  • There are empty headings.
  • Missing or uninformative page title.
  • No page regions are identified.
  • Non-link texts are underlined.
  • Texts overall are very small.
  • Layout tables are present: can cause reading/navigation order issues.

Personas

Next, I created two different personas to represent the archetypes of the interviewed and observed users.


Sofia Randi

Sofia...

  • is a Junior at Brown studying computer science
  • is often busy and doesn't spend long periods of time on meals
  • likes to be healthy and limits her sugar-intake, and generally avoids sugary drinks
  • generally likes having a routine lifestyle and does not like to be out of her comfort zone

Parker Koche

Parker...

  • is a Freshman at Brown who is undecided and loves to explore
  • loves exploring courses in different departments, restaurants in Providence, and new drinks!
  • likes to take time at their meals and enjoy conversations with friends
  • is usually fickle, and their mind is easily changed

Takeaways

Some of the key takeaways and findings of this assignment were...

  • The users of the drink dispenser are generally divided into two groups:
    1. users who appreciate the various, colorful flavors offered, and are willing to explore different flavors
    2. users who simply want to hydrate and are not willing to spend time exploring other options
  • Although the first group of users may greatly benefit from the Exclusive Flavors page and the various filters offered, majority of users do not notice their existence.
  • Many users are repeat users, and are by now accustomed to the flow of the interface. Regardless, all interviewed users found the interface to be intuitive.
